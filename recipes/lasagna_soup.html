Lasagna Soup

Description

If you love lasagna, but also love soup, you're sure to love lasagna soup. It is a different approach to the classic casserole for when you want that taste, but you want soup. Hearty and filling, give this a try.

    Ingredients
  • 1/2 yellow onion, diced (about a cup)
  • 6-8 cloves minced garlic
  • 1/2 lb hot or mild italian sausage
  • 1/2 lb of lean ground beef
  • 2 Tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 tsp Tony's seasoning (optional)
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp parsley
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 24 oz marinara sauce (RAO's recommended)
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • 4-6 cups of chicken or veggie stock, depending on how soupy you want it
  • 1/2 lb of lasagna noodles, broken into pieces
  • Handful of fresh basil

    Ricotta Topping:
  • 1 cup parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/4 cup parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    Directions:
  1. In a medium bowl, combine your parsley, ricotta, mozzarella, parmesan cheese, salt and pepper. Set aside.
  2. In a loarge soup pot or dutch oven, heat your olive oil over medium heat. Add in your onion, add a pinch of salt and cook for 4 minutes. Throw in your garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Add in your sausage and beef, breaking into pieces with a wooden spoon. Cook for a few minutes and then season with your salt, pepper, basil, oregano, parsley, italian seasoning, red pepper, and optional Tony's seasoning. Cook until no longer pink.
  4. Add in your tomato paste and stir. Cook for a couple of minutes. Pour in your broth and marinara sauce. Bring to a boil.
  5. Pour in your lasagna pieces, reduce heat and simmer for about 10 minutes, or until al dente, stirring occasionally.
  6. Once your noodles are done cooking, mix in your heavy cream and fresh basil (torn)
  7. Adjust seasonings if needed. Stir, serve, and top with a dollop of your ricotta mixture.
