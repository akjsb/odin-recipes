Crock Pot Chili

Hearty for when the weather turns slightly cooler, simple to put together, the hardest part is having patience for it to cook while the savory and spicy fragrance fills your house with mouth-watering aromas. Twenty minutes of preparation time, then set it for 4 hours and forget about it (or try) and you'll be on your way to working up your winter layer of insulation. Just kidding, this is pretty lean.

    Ingredients
  • 1 lb lean ground beef
  • 2 medium-sized yellow onions, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 jalapenos, deseeded and deveined
  • (1) 15oz can stewed tomatoes, with juice
  • (2) 15oz cans tomato sauce
  • (1) 4oz can Hatch diced green chiles
  • (1) 15oz can chili spiced beans--I like Texas Ranch Styleâ„¢ beans
  • (1) 16oz can pinto beans, drained
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp fresh ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
    Preparation
  1. Brown the ground beef
  2. Add chili powder and cayenne powder to meat when it is nearly done and continue to stir for 1-2 minutes. Add to crock pot.
  3. Saute onions, jalapeno, and red bell pepper, adding garlic about 1-2 minutes before done, and add to crock pot.
  4. Add 1/2 cup of water to pan used to brown meat and saute vegetables, deglaze, and add to crock pot.
  5. Open all cans and add contents to crock pot
  6. Add oregano to the crock pot
  7. Cover with lid, and cook on high 2 hours (stirring occasionally), then set to low for another 2 hours, or until ready to eat
  8. Enjoy with whatever toppings you prefer. Suggestions include: sour cream, cheddar cheese, chives, diced raw onions, etc.
